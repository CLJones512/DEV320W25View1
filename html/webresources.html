W3Schools


I found this all the way back in my first web design class. It has tutorials for so many different web based languages, and I use it quite a bit to this day. Regardless of whether I need to quickly review a basic concept or learn something completely new, it's always in my back pocket to pull out whenever I need it. As a bonus, it has a nice little editor embedded in all the exercises so I can quickly test something before I try it on my own applications.

Rating: 10 out of 10

Link

GeeksForGeeks


Another all purpose reference, this time for all things programming, web based or otherwise. This one is another great one to keep in your back pocket, although it does have a couple of drawbacks. First off, it's not entirely free the way W3Schools is, so some content is behind a paywall. Also, I found the site a little bit more difficult to navigate, and more often than not I find myself navigating there from Google instead of the site itself. All in all though, it's still a great resource.

Rating: 9 out of 10

Link

Stack Overflow


Ah yes, Stack Overflow. We've all ended up here far more than any of us are willing to admit. To me, this almost feels like a bit of a last resort. Will you get the solution you need? Probably. Will you get a needlessly complicated solution written by someone who's mercilessly judging the original poster? Most definitely. Not the first place I'd go when I need help with something, but more times than not it'll help dig me up.

Rating: 6 out of 10

Link

LinkedIn Learning


This one isn't so much of a resource to go to when I'm in the thick of my work, but rather one to go to when I'm trying to gather information. The videos are very comprehensive, and they teach me all I need to know about various aspects of application development plus so much more. Sometimes the videos can drone on long after I've gotten the point, and it's definitely not a resource to reference on the fly, but it's still good for information gathering.

Rating: 8 out of 10

Link

Web.Dev


This is one that I found very recently. Like LinkedIn learning, this isn't so much of a quick reference type of site, but rather one for spending a while working through the tutorials, as they have a lot of free workshops on HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more. I haven't spent a ton of time yet looking through the site, but they have a very wide range of different classes that I can take in my spare time to help make me a better web developer.

Rating: 8 out of 10
